Our Work

Youth Community

Representation and Empowerment

We build spaces where youth across the Rohingya diaspora can connect and strengthen their ties to the wider Rohingya community. We aim to amplify the voices of this global community, and support youth in recognizing and celebrating each other beyond current media narratives.

Rohingya Language

Learning and Advocacy

TAs mother tongue is a key component to cultural identity, we strongly advocate for the use of the Rohingya language across the diaspora. We aim to normalise the spoken and written forms of the Rohingya language in spaces for Rohingya youth. We are also building various tools and resources to support Rohingya-speaking youth in learning.

Islamic Knowledge

Translations and Accessibility

Religion is foundational to the identity of a Rohingya Muslim. We believe that Islamic knowledge should be accessible in one’s mother tongue, and we strive to support the Islamic education of Rohingya youth by providing translated resources.

Bridging Networks

Community and Allyship

Awareness is a key foundation for the public to participate in championing social justice for the Rohingya. We are building resources and spaces that improve accessibility to information, helping supporters and allies understand the ways they can contribute. We also seek to create spaces that encourage connectedness and collaboration across this work.
